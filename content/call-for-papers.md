## Topics of Interest

We invite submissions on a broad range of topics related to the technology of esports and competitive gaming. These papers will be reviewed by the workshop organizers and invited reviewers if needed.

### Graphics and Rendering

- Real-time rendering techniques for high frame rates (>240 Hz)
- Latency reduction techniques (e.g., NVIDIA Reflex, AMD Anti-Lag)
- Perceptually-based rendering and visual optimization for clarity
- Graphics for broadcast, spectating, and augmented reality overlays
- Novel display technologies for competitive environments

### Systems and Performance

- Low-latency input processing stacks (OS, driver, and hardware levels)
- Network protocols and prediction models for competitive integrity
- Performance analysis, benchmarking, and optimization of game engines
- Hardware co-design for competitive gaming (CPUs, GPUs, peripherals)
- Matchmaking and netcode for low population or remote servers

### Human Factors and HCI

- Psychophysical studies of latency, refresh rate, and motion blur perception
- UI/UX design for high-stakes, information-dense gameplay
- Player performance modeling, metrics, and data analytics
- Haptic feedback and alternative modalities for player input
- Accessibility in competitive gaming
- Spectator-facing UIs for tournaments or livestreaming

### CSCW, Data, and AI

- Tools for collaborative team strategy and communication
- Data visualization for post-game analysis and coaching
- AI-driven coaching systems and training bots
- Computer vision techniques for game state analysis
- Methods for cheat detection and prevention

## Submission Guidelines

All accepted papers will be published in the conference's proceedings as sketches. We welcome both novel research ideas and industry insights that advance our understanding of esports technology.
