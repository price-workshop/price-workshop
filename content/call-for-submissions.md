      <h2>Call for Submissions</h2>
      <p class="lead">Join us for the PRICE workshop at SIGGRAPH 2026</p>
    </div>
    
    <div class="container-sm">
      <p>
        The <strong>Performance, Rendering, and Interaction for Competitive Esports (PRICE)</strong> workshop 
        at SIGGRAPH 2026 brings together researchers, developers, and industry professionals to explore 
        cutting-edge techniques in real-time graphics, performance optimization, and human-computer interaction 
        specifically tailored for competitive esports environments.
      </p>
      
      <p>
        We invite submissions using the ACM single-column format of 4-8 pages (excluding references) on all related topics, 
        including the following:
      </p>
      
      <h3>Graphics and Rendering:</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Real-time rendering techniques for high frame rates (>240 Hz)</li>
        <li>Latency reduction techniques (e.g., NVIDIA Reflex, AMD Anti-Lag)</li>
        <li>Perceptually-based rendering and visual optimization for clarity</li>
        <li>Graphics for broadcast, spectating, and augmented reality overlays</li>
        <li>Display technologies for competitive environments</li>
        <li>Rendering techniques for enhancing perception of competitive gameplay elements</li>
      </ul>
      
      <h3>Systems and Performance:</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Low-latency input processing stacks (OS, driver, and hardware levels)</li>
        <li>Network protocols and prediction models for competitive integrity</li>
        <li>Performance analysis, benchmarking, and optimization of game engines</li>
        <li>Hardware co-design for competitive gaming (CPUs, GPUs, peripherals)</li>
        <li>Matchmaking and netcode for low population (user count) or remote servers</li>
      </ul>
      
      <h3>Human Factors and HCI:</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Psychophysical studies of latency, refresh rate, and motion blur perception</li>
        <li>UI/UX design for high-stakes, information-dense gameplay</li>
        <li>Player performance modeling, metrics, and data analytics</li>
        <li>Haptic feedback and alternative modalities for player input</li>
        <li>Accessibility in competitive gaming</li>
        <li>Spectator-facing UIs for tournaments or livestreaming</li>
        <li>Understanding and mitigating social and emotional pressures impacting performance</li>
        <li>Analyzing and addressing bias and antisocial behavior</li>
      </ul>
      
      <h3>CSCW, Data, and AI:</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Tools for collaborative team strategy and communication</li>
        <li>Data visualization for post-game analysis and coaching</li>
        <li>AI-driven coaching systems and training bots</li>
        <li>Computer vision techniques for game state analysis</li>
        <li>Methods for cheat detection and prevention</li>
        <li>Novel and emergent impacts of GenAI on gaming and players</li>
      </ul>
      
      <div class="alert alert-info" style="margin: 2rem 0;">
        <h4>Important Notes:</h4>
        <ul>
          <li>All submissions must be in <strong>ACM single-column format</strong> (4-8 pages excluding references)</li>
          <li>Submissions must be in <strong>PDF format</strong></li>
          <li>Submissions must be <strong>anonymous</strong> for review</li>
          <li>Abstracts are due April 15, 2026</li>
          <li>Camera-ready papers are due May 15, 2026</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
